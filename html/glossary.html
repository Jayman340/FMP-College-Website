Glossary

Definitions of key cybersecurity terms used in the timeline.

Worm

A type of malware that can spread by itself across computers and networks without needing user interaction.

Ransomware

Malware that locks files or systems and demands payment to restore access.

Botnet

A group of infected computers that are controlled remotely by an attacker.

Social Engineering

Tricking people into giving away information or opening harmful files.

Patch

An update that fixes security problems or bugs in software.

Vulnerability

A weakness in a system that attackers can exploit.

Supply Chain Attack

When attackers target a trusted company or software to reach other organisations.

Data Extortion

Stealing data and threatening to release it unless a payment is made.

DDoS Attack

When many systems are used to overload a website or service so it stops working.

SMTP (Simple Mail Transfer Protocol)

A standard protocol used to send emails between servers. Some malware, such as Mydoom, uses its own SMTP system to send large numbers of emails automatically without needing a normal email client.

Peer-to-Peer (P2P) File Sharing

A method of sharing files directly between computers over a network, without a central server. Malware can spread through P2P networks by copying itself into shared folders where other users may download infected files.
